Composite index = weighted sum of sign-adjusted StandardScaler outputs. Burden vars inverted (×−1). Weights: rent burden 20%, home value/income 20%, mortgage burden 15%, rent/income 15%, poverty ratio 10%, unemployment 10%, income 5%, rent 2.5%, home value 2.5%.

Three expert regressors trained on 9 StandardScaler features with 5-fold cross-validation.
Expert 1
Ridge Regression
Linear model with L2 regularization (α=1.0). Fast, interpretable coefficients. Assumes linear relationship between features and index.
StrengthCoefficients = direct weights
WeaknessMisses non-linear interactions
Hyperparamsalpha=1.0
Expert 2
Random Forest
200 trees, max_depth=8, min_samples_leaf=3. Captures non-linear interactions and feature importance via impurity. Robust to outliers.
StrengthFeature importance scores
WeaknessCan overfit small datasets
Hyperparamsn_est=200, depth=8
Expert 3
Gradient Boosting
200 boosting stages, max_depth=4, learning_rate=0.05. Sequential error correction. Often highest accuracy but slowest to train.
StrengthHighest predictive accuracy
WeaknessRequires careful tuning
Hyperparamslr=0.05, depth=4
